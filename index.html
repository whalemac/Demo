Paragraphs are the building blocks of papers. Many students define paragraphs i n terms of length: a paragraph is a group of at least five sentences, a paragraph i
s half a page long, etc. In reality, though, the unity and coherence of ideas among s entences is what constitutes a paragraph. A paragraph is defined as “a group
of sentences or a single sentence that forms a unit” (Lunsford and Connors 116). Length and appearance do not determine whether a section in a paper is a paragraph. For instance, in some styles imately, a paragraph is a sentence or group of sentences that support one main
idea. In this handout, we will refer to this as the “co
ntrolling idea,” because it controls what happens in the rest of the paragraph
